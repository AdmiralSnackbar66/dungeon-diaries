# Exams
## player knowledge
With this I will prepare normally a 4-5 multiple choice exam regarding the exam material. While this breaks the 4th wall a little my players love being tested on weird facts (and I’ll throw in one or two easy questions).

To score this I just calculate their normal grade correct/total questions. This will be their base grade

## character knowledge
With this I have a character roll an ability check relating to the exam (I normally pick 3-5 relevant abilities and let them choose) and if they studied for the exam they get advantage.

To score this I use the following breakdown:

1-9 = remove one correct answer from base score

10 = keep base score the same

11-15 = add one correct answer to base score

16+ = add two correct answers to base score

## dumb luck or cheating

This section is optional for the students. But, if they are not happy with their score they can try to get lucky or they can cheat (I don’t let them do both just to limit how easy getting a good grade is)

I use the following breakdown for luck:

1 - remove two correct answers

2-9 - remove one correct answer

10 - nothing

11-19 - add one correct answer

20 - add two correct answers

Now lastly, for cheating. If a student decides to cheat I first have them roll stealth against the professors perception (they get advantage if they planned to cheat ahead of time). If they loose this stealth check then I RP a scenario where their character got caught cheating, which depending on the professor could be really bad.

If they succeed on cheating I let them roll a d20

1 - nothing

2-9 - add one correct answer

10-19 - add two correct answers

20 - add three correct answer
